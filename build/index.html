Google Ventures

Daniel Burka & Jake Knapp

Talk was based on prototyping and building a product in five days.

Key Points & Quotes

Create “Time Pressure” Stop procastinators, motivate people.

Find more than one solution, if the main fails, somsthing to fall back on.

Everyone brainstorms, everyone.

Submit ideas (Post-its) and then people vote. No discussion, this approach removes over-discussion and frees people up to be honest with their views.

Practical uses for eBusiness?

I can see the brainstorm and voting idea working for large projects with different angles and opinions. Having everyone inbolved may pose a problem but I think it's something we should strive towards, team spirit, valuing opinions etc

Workshop - Wednesday

neo.com

Jeff Gothelf & Josh Seiden

Workshop Topic

A deep dive into Lean UX, in particular 'Hypothesis Writing'.

To quote the description of the workshop:

“No one admits it but too often, requirements are just guesses. To cover up this uncomfortable fact, software managers do a vast amount to work on requirements: they pre-plan in painstaking detail, outlining every possible scenario, use case, back-end integration point, and business rule. The end result of all this planning? Too often, itâ€™s disappointment, frustration, and vast sums of wasted money” .

We were broken up into teams, providid with post-its and Sharpies and worked through an excerise outlining user types, scenarioes, features and benefits etc.

At the end of the excersise we had identified a number of user types, their requirements and how the product would target these needs.

Practical uses for eBusiness?

Ties into Agile so very useful.

Workshop leaders have the following book, Lean UX, which I will be buying.

Despite not having 1st hand contact with our user base I think an excerise like this will help us identify the user needs of the project, quicker and clearer, help remove assumptions and produce a superior end product.
